Every prediction, signal, and recommendation in Vexel Horizon carries an **AI Confidence score** — a percentage that tells you how much statistical weight the network's neural models place behind that output. Rather than presenting signals as certainties, Vexel surfaces the underlying probability so you can make informed decisions.

## What AI Confidence measures

AI Confidence is derived from multiple neural network models running in parallel across The Mesh. Each model independently analyzes the same market conditions, and the resulting confidence score reflects how tightly those models agree — in statistical terms, how minimal the standard deviation is across predictions.

The models factor in:

- **News sentiment** — the aggregate tone of relevant market news and social signals
- **Order volume** — the depth and direction of order flow for the asset
- **Historical volatility** — how predictably the asset has behaved under similar conditions in the past

A high score means the models converge strongly on the same outcome. A lower score means greater disagreement among the models, which translates to more uncertainty in the signal.

## Interpreting the score

| Score range | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| 95% and above | Very high confidence — strong consensus across neural models |
| 80% – 94% | Moderate confidence — models broadly agree, with some divergence |
| Below 80% | Lower certainty — exercise additional caution before acting |

<Tip>
  Use the AI Confidence score as one input among several, not as the sole basis for a decision. A 98% confidence reading on a single asset is meaningful, but it is most useful when combined with your own analysis of the underlying market conditions.
</Tip>

## Real-time updates

The Dashboard AI Confidence card is kept current by the `marketPulse` event, which The Mesh emits every 15 seconds. Each pulse carries a `confidence` field that Vexel converts to a percentage and writes directly to the display. You do not need to refresh the page — the value updates automatically.

<Note>
  AI Confidence is a statistical measure of model agreement, not a guarantee of outcome. Market conditions can shift rapidly in ways that fall outside historical patterns. Always conduct your own research before executing trades.
</Note>
