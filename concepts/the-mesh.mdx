The Mesh is the foundation everything in Vexel Horizon runs on. Rather than relying on a central server that a single party could take offline, The Mesh is a network of distributed nodes that collectively process AI calculations, maintain data integrity, and broadcast synchronized market state to every connected terminal worldwide.

## Three core functions

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Parallel Processing" icon="server">
    AI calculations are split across Mesh nodes for ultra-fast, fault-tolerant execution. No single node is a bottleneck or point of failure.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Incorruptibility" icon="shield">
    Data cannot be altered without consensus from the majority of the network. No individual actor — including Vexel — can tamper with recorded state.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mesh Pulse" icon="signal">
    Every 15 seconds, The Mesh emits a heartbeat that synchronizes all prices and governance states across every connected node worldwide.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How The Mesh affects your experience

The Mesh Pulse is the mechanism behind Vexel's real-time updates. When a pulse is received, the `marketPulse` Socket.io event fires in your browser and immediately refreshes three values on your Dashboard:

- **Portfolio value** — your total net worth recalculated against current market prices
- **Yield Velocity** — your per-second capital growth rate, updated to the latest pulse data
- **AI Confidence score** — the network-wide neural confidence level for the current market state

Between pulses, Vexel interpolates your portfolio value locally so the display stays smooth rather than jumping every 15 seconds.

## Status indicators

### Mesh Latency indicator

The top header of the terminal shows a live **Mesh Latency** reading (for example, "Mesh Latency: 14ms"). The indicator dot next to it reflects the pulse cycle:

- **Green** — the indicator turns green for half a second immediately after a pulse is received
- **Blue** — returns to blue as the terminal waits for the next pulse

### Sovereign session indicator

In the sidebar, your user area shows a small green dot next to your name when your session is connected to The Mesh. If that dot is absent, your Sovereign session is not active.

<Note>
  The sidebar session indicator and the header Mesh Latency indicator are independent signals. The session indicator reflects your authentication state; the header indicator reflects live Mesh connectivity.
</Note>

## Fail-Safe Sandbox Mode

<Warning>
  If The Mesh connection is lost, Vexel automatically enters **Fail-Safe Sandbox Mode**. In this state, historical data remains visible but live updates — including price changes, Yield Velocity, and AI Confidence — pause until the connection is restored. You will see a feed entry noting the connection loss.
</Warning>

When connectivity returns, the `marketPulse` stream resumes and all live values update immediately with the latest pulse data.
